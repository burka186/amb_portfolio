DEJA VU



Medium: Processing


I have vivid memories of my childhood, even my very early childhood, but in these memories, I never see myself as I existed then (as a child or a baby). It’s always very unsettling to experience my own memories ‘once removed’ through photos and home movies because in these representations, I am at once at home with myself and strange to myself.


I wanted to evoke this sense of simultaneous strangeness and recognition through the idea of purposeful glitching. I created a glitching program in Processing that takes screen caps from my family’s home movies and shuffles them into and out of focus to create strange new pictures and reveal hidden patterns and beauty. This was a way for me to confront the discomfort I feel at seeing myself on screen, and to find a new appreciation in these familiar memories and images.
