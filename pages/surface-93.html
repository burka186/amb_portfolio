SURFACE MAGAZINE ISSUE 93


IDEAS IN DESIGN: Hey, Buddy, Touching Costs You Extra


For Washington D.C.’s Digital Capital Week in November, local architects Hiroshi Jacobs, Jon Grinham, and Kash Bennett designed an installation that was, in Jacobs’s words, “something more than a static art piece.” The finished product, called Play It Forward, had an undulating polyethylene surface and motion-activated LED light that invited revelers to touch it. Each time a partygoer interacted with the piece, money was automatically donated to charity. A smartphone app tracked the total.
